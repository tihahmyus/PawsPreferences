Dry Food (Kibble)

Easy to store, long shelf life, and usually cheaper. Choose high-quality kibble (high protein, low carbs) to keep your cat healthy.

Wet Food (Canned)

Contains more water → helps with hydration. Often preferred by cats because of aroma & texture.

Raw Diet (BARF)

Based on raw meat. Requires strict hygiene & balanced nutrition. Not suitable for everyone due to bacterial risks.

Homemade Meals

You can cook for your cat, but follow balanced recipes. Avoid just rice + fish. Consult a vet for special menus.

Chocolate & Caffeine

Contains theobromine & caffeine which are toxic to cats.

Onion & Garlic

Can damage red blood cells & cause anemia.

Grapes & Raisins

Harmful to kidneys even in small amounts.

Cooked Bones

Can splinter & cause internal injuries or blockages.

Cow’s Milk

Many cats are lactose intolerant causing diarrhea & stomach upset.

Important Tips

✔ Always provide fresh water
✔ Feed on a schedule, not free feeding all the time
✔ Choose food according to age & health (kitten, adult, senior, special diet)
